Editorial (On Partnerships)

Everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler - Albert Einstein.

Data extraction is always the most tedious step when it comes to quantitative commerce optimization. Small companies usually don’t have the IT resources while large ones struggle with their own complexity.

The bad news is that this step cannot be avoided. Worse, botching it with an improper extraction leads to results that bring more harm than good through “garbage in, garbage out” patterns.

In order to achieve simplicity and reliability, we have fostered a network of software partners who offer integrations with Salescast. Multichannel Order Managers (MOMs) such as Brightpearl, eCommHub or Linnworks now provide tutorials to extract data from their systems into files compatible with Salescast. Other partners such as Webgility, Megaventory or SkuVault go even further by offering native seamless integrations.

In a nutshell, whether you’re using Quickbooks, Magento, Shopify, BigCommerce, or a combination of these, or if you’re selling on marketplaces such as eBay or Amazon, inventory optimization through Salescast has never been easier.

Joannes Vermorel, Founder of Lokad


BotDefender: protect your store from the automated retrieval of your prices by competitors

Over the past few years, competitive intelligence tools able to retrieve all the prices of a web store on a daily basis have grown from expensive tools, affordable only by the largest retailers, to commoditized technologies accessible even to very small merchants. This technology is known as web scraping; it uses robots to automatically visit all the pages of a targeted site. With this technology on the rise, the market pressure for retailers has been increasing significantly.

Several of our customers were reporting that they were outpriced by competitors because they were having all their prices monitored in near real time. We finally decided to give them the technology to fight back and BotDefender was born.

BotDefender protects your online store from those robots with a near-zero performance impact (see our video). It comes with a free Express Plan that does not expire, and to make things dead simple, we’ve just released a free add-on for Magento, co-designed with Wyomind.


Why classic forecasts can't compete with quantile forecasts

Not all forecasting methods are born equal. As far as inventory optimization is concerned, classic forecasting methods tend to make no sense whatsoever. It’s not a matter of having the right models, or of properly tuning those models, it’s a matter of perspective.

Classic forecasts are not tailored for retail and commerce. The classic forecasting theory was designed and refined to deal with objects fundamentally different from commerce, such as weather or demography. This theory is neither outdated nor incorrect, but it is simply not a good fit for commerce. In contrast, quantile forecasts deeply embrace the commerce viewpoint and the essential notions of lead time and service level. Promotion planning – Data, processes and optimization challenges

Forecasting is generally considered as a difficult exercise, but there is one area in general merchandise retail considered as even more complicated than the rest: promotion planning.

When it comes to inventory optimization, Lokad generally favors the development of on-shelf solutions - such as Salescast - but in this particular area, we must admit that forecasting depends too much on the specificities and processes of the companies for a packaged solution to be completely satisfactory.

Based on our hands-on experience, helping large retail networks improve their promotion forecasts, we have published a guidebook of sorts, divided in three parts. It provides pointers to the many challenges that one can expect to face in terms of data, processes and optimization.
