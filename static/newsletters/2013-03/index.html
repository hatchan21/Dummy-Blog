Phantomscan, get rid of phantom inventory

The quasi-totality of supply-chain related software such as ERP, MRP or WMS assumes that inventory records are correct when computing reorder quantities; and yet, studies show that inaccurate inventory records are a widespread problem. At the store level, the problem is sometimes so intense that less than half of the records are actually correct. Garbage in, Garbage out.

Phantomscan was designed by Lokad in order to address this concern. The name of the app has been coined after the term phantom inventory that colloquially refers to inventory that exists in the system, but that has no physical counterparts.

The traditional approach for getting rid of inaccurate inventory records is cycle counting. While those audit policies can improve inventory accuracy, they are very time consuming when performed the usual way; hence, the price for a marginal improvement of inventory accuracy is steep.

Phantomscan, in contrast, leverages advance statistical analysis to fine-tune the prioritization of inventory controls in order to make the most of every minute spent counting, starting with items that need to be recounted the most. Phantomscan predicts the accuracy of each inventory record.

Salescast, inventory optimization with TSV files

Moving data from your company's IT systems to Lokad is typically the most time consuming step when getting started with our inventory optimization webapp Salescast. Since its launch, Salescast has been supporting SQL data imports. This approach has many upsides, such as offering a great level of resilience against data corruption. However, as a downside, this approach requires some expert knowledge about database administration that was not always available.

Therefore, we introduced a new data format: flat text files formatted as TSV (tab separated values). This format is compatible with Excel, and, therefore, it becomes possible to obtain a forecast report on Excel sheets, provided that a TSV export is made beforehand.

Then, in order to push those TSV files toward Lokad, we have introduced a file hosting service that comes as an add-on for Salescast. This service supports FTP (File Transfer Protocol), which is a widely accepted standard to move files around on the Internet. Like all apps that are designed by Lokad, this app is based on Windows Azure (the cloud computing platform of Microsoft) for better scalability and reliability.

Data Platform, your first step toward Big Data

In many industries such as retail, wholesale or hospitality, software represents the primary driver of today's and tomorrow's innovation. However, as the IT division is frequently the only division responsible for software matters, this division ends up being responsible for handling the bulk of the innovation that happens within the company. This is a very heavy burden, and no matter how talented the staff might be, the IT division soon becomes the bottleneck of all changes within the company.

The Data Platform is an open source project that precisely addresses this major organizational problem. In short, this platform aims to make the (raw) data of the company self-servicing. By offering data in self-service to all divisions, IT can disentangle itself from the structural bottleneck position. Indeed, non-IT divisions can then suddenly start iteration over the data on their own.

Unlike Business Intelligence, the Data Platform is not tailored in order to gain insights, but rather to gain access to the data in a relatively unrefined format. The Data Platform provides no intelligence, only data. This ensures that the effort to setup the platform remains low. Delivering expertise over the data falls back to the relevant divisions, relieving IT in the process.
